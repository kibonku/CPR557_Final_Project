### **Issues and Solutions**

#### **1. Coordinate System Definition**

To ensure consistent transformations, the world coordinate system is defined as follows:

* **X-axis (Horizontal):** Left/Right relative to the screen.
* **Y-axis (Vertical):** Up/Down, aligned with the growth direction of the stem.
* **Z-axis (Depth):** In/Out relative to the screen (Camera direction).
* **Zoom In/Out:** Movement of the camera along the Z-axis.
* **Panning:** Translation along the World X and Y axes.


#### **2. Resolving the "Seesaw Effect" in Leaf Rotation**

* **Problem:** Applying `rotation.z` to the `leavesGroup` caused a "seesaw" motion where leaves on the +X side moved down while leaves on the -X side moved up. This occurred because the leaves are distributed around the Y-axis pivot; a rigid body rotation around Z converts horizontal offset into vertical displacement.
* **Solution:** Leaf distribution must be managed via **Y-axis rotation** (e.g., 0°, 120°, 240° from a top-down view) to ensure they radiate correctly from the stem.


#### **3. Defense Logic: GPU Vertex Deformation**

This implementation utilizes a standard technique for simulating wind in real-time graphics.

* **Mechanical Analogy:** The leaf is modeled as a **cantilever beam** fixed at one end. Under a lateral load (wind), displacement increases relative to the distance from the fixed point.
* **Implementation:** We use `weight = clamp(position.x, 0.0, 1.0)` as a linear approximation of this deflection.
* **Defense Statement:** *"The deformation is a linear approximation of cantilever beam deflection under lateral wind load. The root vertex is anchored (weight=0), the tip receives maximum displacement (weight=1), and intermediate vertices are interpolated smoothly to maintain structural integrity."*

#### **4. Preventing Axial Stretching**

* **Problem:** Simply moving vertices in the direction of the wind can cause the leaf mesh to stretch unnaturally along its length.
* **Solution (Perpendicular Method):** Compute wind-induced displacement as a vector **perpendicular** to the leaf's local tangent direction. This allows the leaf to bend realistically without changing its surface area or length.

#### **5. Advanced Wind Control Logic**

* **Vector-based Direction:** Mouse position alone is not a direction. Direction is calculated as the vector from the initial click point ($P_{start}$) to the current drag position ($P_{current}$).
* **W Key Down:** Capture $P_{start}$.
* **W Key Drag:** Calculate `windVector = P_current - P_start`.
* **W Key Up:** Reset wind to zero.


* **Scaling:** A `kWindScale` constant is applied to prevent extreme deformations when the mouse is dragged far from the origin.

#### **6. Hardware Execution: Vulkan on Apple Silicon**

* **GPU Presence:** MacBook M-series chips (M1/M2/M3/M4) feature an integrated Apple Silicon GPU.
* **GPU Deformation:** Unlike CPU-based calculations, GPU deformation executes within the **Vertex Shader**. The GPU processes thousands of vertices in parallel every frame to calculate new positions based on the `windPos` passed via the `GlobalUBO`.
* **Compatibility:** On macOS, Vulkan commands are translated to Metal via **MoltenVK**, allowing high-performance hardware acceleration directly on the Apple GPU.
