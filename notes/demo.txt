BEFORE YOU START
----------------
1. Build:  ./compile-unx.bat or ./compile-win.bat
2. Run:    ./FinalPorject.exe   
3. App starts in Navigation Mode showing the plant scene.
   Terminal will print the control key summary.

---------------------------------------------------
OPENING – Project Introduction  (~20 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
SAY:     "My final project is an interactive 3D plant modeling application
          built on Vulkan.

          The core idea is to let the user design a custom pot by placing
          Bezier control points, while a pre-built plant — stem and three
          leaves — sits above it, all connected through a hierarchical
          scene graph.

          It demonstrates seven phases: Bezier surface construction,
          interactive surface of revolution, scene graph with parent-child transforms, 
          camera navigation, real-time wind simulation,
          Bezier control-point animation, and texture mapping as the stretch goal."

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 1 – Plant Scene Overview  (~45 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
ACTION:  The app opens. The plant (stem + 3 leaves) is visible. No pot yet.
SAY:     "The application loads a pre-built plant scene on startup (hardcoded control points).
          The stem and pot are a Bezier revolution surface — a 2D profile
          revolved around an axis with analytic normals.
          The three leaves are flat parametric meshes (_makeFlatLeafNode)
          — width, camber(beam), and droop are each controlled by Bezier control points.
          All four nodes are organized in a scene graph hierarchy.
          The pot slot is empty for now — I will build it live in edit mode."

What	                        Reality
At startup	                stem + 3 leaves only — no pot
Pre-built revolution surface	stem only
User-built revolution surface	pot (Phase 2, edit mode)
Node count at startup	        4 visible nodes (stem, leaf1, leaf2, leaf3)

ACTION:  Press F to fit-all so the whole plant is centered.
ACTION:  Hold R and drag the mouse slowly to orbit around the plant.
SAY:     "Rotating the camera shows the stem and three leaves
          arranged at 120-degree intervals around the stem axis."

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 2 – Scene Graph Traversal  (~45 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
ACTION:  Press Tab to print the scene graph to the terminal.
         Show the terminal output briefly.
SAY:     "Here is the scene graph hierarchy:
          plant_root → user_pot, stem, leaves_group → leaf1, leaf2, leaf3.
          user_pot has no model yet — its slot is reserved for the pot
          I will build in edit mode. Parent transforms propagate to all children."

ACTION:  Press N several times, showing each node name printed in the terminal.
         The currently selected node will spin visually.
SAY:     "Pressing N cycles through each node in depth-first order.
          The selected node spins so we can see which one is active.
          When a group node is selected, the whole subtree moves with it —
          that is the parent-child transform at work."

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 3 – Wind Response  (~45 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
ACTION:  Hold W and move the mouse slowly to the left. Leaves bend left.
ACTION:  Move the mouse to the right. Leaves bend right.
ACTION:  Move the mouse up. Leaves bend upward.
SAY:     "Holding W and moving the mouse sets the wind direction.
          The wind vector is passed to the GPU each frame via a uniform buffer.
          In the vertex shader, each leaf vertex is displaced by
          windPos times a weight equal to clamp(position.x, 0, 1) —
          so the root of the leaf is anchored and the tip deflects maximally.
          This is a linear approximation of cantilever beam deflection."

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 4 – Camera Navigation  (~30 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
ACTION:  Press P, drag to pan the scene left and right.
ACTION:  Press Z, drag to zoom in close to the pot.
ACTION:  Press T, drag to roll/twist the camera.
ACTION:  Press F to snap back to fit-all.
SAY:     "Camera navigation includes rotate, pan, zoom, twist, and 
          fit-all carried over from HW4 and corrected for this project."

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 5 – Bezier Surface Editor  (~60 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
ACTION:  Press SPACE to switch to Edit Mode.
         Screen switches to orthographic, 2D view.
SAY:     "Pressing SPACE enters edit mode. In edit mode I can interactively
          define Bezier control points and generate a revolution surface."

ACTION:  Click 5–6 points in the upper half of the screen to form a profile
         (e.g., click from left to right, making a gentle curve like a vase).
SAY:     "Each click places a control point. The Bezier curve is shown in
          white (press G); control points in red with a yellow polygon."

ACTION:  Press B to build the revolution surface.
SAY:     "Pressing B generates the surface of revolution by rotating the
          Bezier profile around the X-axis using the analytic normal formula."

ACTION:  Press Shift+N to toggle normals.
SAY:     "The green lines are the surface normals used in Phong shading."

ACTION:  Press G to toggle color gradient ON.
SAY:     "The rainbow gradient maps hue to the axial parameter u."

ACTION:  Press X to toggle helical twist.
SAY:     "Twist adds a full helical rotation as the surface revolves."

ACTION:  Press SPACE to return to Navigation Mode.
ACTION:  Orbit around the user-built surface to show it in 3D.

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 6 – Texture Mapping (Stretch Goal)  (~30 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
ACTION:  In Navigation Mode, orbit slowly around the pot and then the leaves.
SAY:     "The stretch goal — texture mapping — is fully implemented.
          The pot samples a PNG image texture loaded via stb_image and
          uploaded to a Vulkan image sampler.
          The leaves and stem use procedural GPU textures written in GLSL:
          the leaf shader computes a three-stop axial gradient, a tapered
          midrib, curved lateral veins with quadratic bend, sub-veins,
          and high-frequency micro-surface variation — all per fragment.
          The stem shader adds vertical stripes, horizontal node bands,
          and a highlight strip.
          The fragment shader selects the mode per object via a flag
          packed into push constants."

---------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 7 – Wrap-up  (~15 sec)
---------------------------------------------------
SAY:     "To summarize: the application demonstrates Bezier curve evaluation,
          surface-of-revolution generation with analytic normals, a hierarchical
          scene graph with DFS traversal, corrected camera navigation,
          real-time GPU vertex deformation for wind simulation,
          and full texture mapping — both image-based and procedural —
          as the stretch goal."

ACTION:  Press F to show the full plant one final time.

"How does texture mapping work?"
Download a texture image from https://ambientcg.com/
The fragment shader receives a flag in push.normalMatrix[3][2]: 1=sample pot_texture.png from a Vulkan sampler, 2=run the procedural leaf GLSL function, 3=run the procedural stem function.

"How does wind work?"
Wind direction is a vec2 in the GlobalUBO. The vertex shader displaces each leaf vertex by windPos * weight, where weight = clamp(position.x, 0, 1) — a linear approximation of cantilever beam deflection.

"How does the scene graph enforce parent-child transforms?"
DFS traversal accumulates a world matrix by multiplying parent transforms down the chain before drawing each node. Moving leaves_group moves all three leaves together.
